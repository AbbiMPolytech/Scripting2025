Halifax welcomes all to view their beautiful destinations and attractions. Rich with sights to see, visit and take a walk through the public gardens where whimsical fences surround ponds and growing greenery with accessible walking paths and picnic benches are placed throughout the park to sit and take in the water fountains and gazebo highlights the center of the park. Feeling hungry? Visit the downtown waterfront, where food stands and tourism thrive. There are many choices from Cow's famous ice cream to Smoke's Poutinerie. Take a walk up and down to see buskers and the big wave or reach the end of the pier to find the discovery Centre, filled with different science attractions and experiments to conduct yourself.

Or check out the farmers market right next door with independent stalls and local vendors to pick up your pick from. Down the road from the waterfront you will find the public library, The Art Gallery of Nova Scotia, and the Canadian Historical site Citadel Hill. As well Peggys cove is just a 40-minute drive from downtown Halifax. And the beautiful fisherman's town, Lunenburg, is just an hour away. Lots to see and do when you visit, it is too much, and you just must move there to see it all!
