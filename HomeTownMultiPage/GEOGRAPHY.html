Halifax has a unique and diverse geography, in its coastal location, following the glacial history, and rugged natural landscapes. Halifax is a destination where you will find everything you will need in your outdoor pursuits. The city's coastline is marked by rocky headlands, coves, and beaches. Inland, the terrain consists of rolling hills, lakes, forests, and wetlands. Many of Halifax's lakes and rivers, such as Chocolate Lake, Long Lake and Sackville River, are important recreational and ecological areas. Halifax's climate is determined by the Atlantic Ocean, resulting in fluctuating warm summers, but wet and stormy winters, topped with frequent fog, especially along the coast, experiencing hurricanes, flash floods, and prominent levels of snow in this area.

Life takes many forms in this diverse land, Halifax is home to many wildlife such as raccoons, deer, foxes, sea birds, and even marine life like whales and sometimes dolphins in warmer months. Modern urban areas continue to grow branching out of Halifax's main center; suburban areas continue to flourish, Darthmouth, Bedford and Sackville further out of the city. Overall, Halifax's blends marine and terrestrial features, shaping the city's culture, economy, transportation, and lifestyle. Its natural harbor, rugged coastline, and diverse inland landscapes make it one of Canada's most distinctive geographic regions.
