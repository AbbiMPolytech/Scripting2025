The economic structure of Halifax is supported by many diverse fields, including shipping and the Halifax port, seasonal tourism, public services such as education, healthcare, and essential trades. One of the most important sectors in Halifax is the port industry, as it handles container traffic, cruise ships, naval vessels, and cargo shipments. The finance and insurance sector is also important with companies in downtown Halifax. The city also has large economic benefits from the strong tourism industry, seasonal and year-round; Halifax continues to be supported by its historic sites, coastal scenery, festivals, and cruise ship arrivals. Halifax has also grown in education, with multiple universities and colleges drawing students from across Canada and further boosting housing, retail, and services.

Another part of Halifax's economic structure that has been growing in recent years is the ocean and technology field, home to many ocean research facilities, technology startups, and marine companies working in fields such ocean mapping, robotics, and environmental monitoring and studies. As of September 2025, it was reported that 60% of the population above 15 is employed. With a growing population and advances in technology and infrastructure, the employment rate is likely to continue to grow, as it will provide more jobs continuing to build Nova Scotia's economic footprint.
