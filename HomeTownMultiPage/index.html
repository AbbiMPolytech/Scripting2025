Founded in 1749, Halifax is the capital city of Nova Scotia, Atlantic Canada. With 13,300km of coastline, forests and many lakes lining neighborhoods and highways, Halifax offers a lot for its visitors and residents. Known for its rich marine and native history, as well as its famous tourism opportunities and attractions. Halifax effortlessly blends small town charm with modern urban hubs. Home to many young and old, Halifax offers a lifetime of opportunity to explore, grow and live freely.

As the capital of Nova Scotia, Halifax plays a significant role, hosting the economic, education and arts scene of the province. On a drive through town, you will immediately recognize the effortless blend of historical and modern architecture, showing the ever-ending growth of the small city. Filled with life, the people range from young to old, and everything in between, from making friends in kindergarten to completing education, entering the workforce, and retiring generation after generation; life is full circle in Halifax. Many distinct cultures are welcomed, making it a diverse and busy city with opportunities for all. Halifax continues to grow and expand its horizons for the future of its citizens. As it continues to learn from its history and grow into its future, Halifax celebrates its communities and people day after day.
