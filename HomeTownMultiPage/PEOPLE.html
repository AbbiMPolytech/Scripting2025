Halifax is home to many diverse backgrounds and ethicizes that total the estimated population of 530,000. The 2021 Census profile reports the average age for individuals was between 15 and 65 and the top minority being Black and South Asian individuals. The census also found the most spoken, non-official languages are Arabic, German, and Chinese with most still fluent in English. Originally owned by the Mi'kmaq people, the providence works diligently towards reconciliation and growth for both parties.

Living by the Ocean shapes the culture in many ways, most of the population loving outdoor activities and including activity to everyday life. People regularly enjoy the hikes and paths placed throughout local and national parks; many lakes ready for swimming and boating and beaches never too far away; winter skiing and skating are popular too. With a booming communities, the people of Halifax are very friendly and welcoming to new and visiting people; neighbors are well known to each other and speak regularly. Most of the population comes from senior citizens, young adults and children, newcomers moving to town for work and or education, generational Scotians, and military work (Halifax being home to Canadas larges Navy base). In public settings, people are polite and respectful of others, keeping Halifax a safe place and inclusive space for all.
