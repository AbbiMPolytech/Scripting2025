Show Overview

House is a medical drama following Greg House, famously known as “House.” He and his diagnostic team treat patients with rare illnesses, often racing against time as conditions worsen. Each episode begins with House encountering a patient with abnormal symptoms. He quickly comes up with a diagnosis but must adapt as new symptoms appear, House and his team strive to save the patient's life before the episode ends.

The show was inspired by the characters of Sherlock Holmes and his cases, using critical thinking and unconventional ways of finding answers. Further, the show has many parallels to Sherlock, with his best friend's name being James Wilson, like Watson, his apartment being 221B, addiction problems and a prior love interest named Irene Adler. Even Houses name, where Holmes (sounds like home) is made into “House”.

The show ran from November 2004 to May 2012. It consists of eight seasons, each with about 23 episodes. Over the series, the team sees around 175 patients, only losing 18, aside from two intentional deaths and one patient who was already dead.

Reaching 71 countries, it was the world's most-watched show in 2008. House remained in the US top ten for 4 years and was Fox's most popular show after American Idol. House M.D was nominated for 25 Emmys and won five. It was nominated for nine Golden Globes and won two. It was nominated for 13 People's Choice Awards and collected nine. The show received many more awards as well.

The show continues to be loved by many. Reports of medical students choosing diagnostics because of House's (Hugh Laurie) exploration and influence as the beloved doctor.

Character Overview - Gregory House

The show follows the main character House. Certified as a diagnostician specializing in Infectious diseases and nephrology. His egotistical, narcissistic, and misanthropic personality brought viewers to the edge of their seat, always wondering what he would do next to cure his patient. He led the show with a sense of humor and sarcasm many loved.

He was Born in 1959, to homemaker Blythe and marine pilot John House his father being in active duty causing House to live in various places throughout his childhood. Surviving a rough childhood with both mental and physical abuse, as well as forms of emotional neglect. The result leads to him struggling with relationships and isolating himself from the many chances of romantic and platonic connections House grew up as a smart child, spending free time diving into various interests, such as chemistry, piano and many more. This led him to pursue medicine, attending Maryland University and John Hopkins for, achieving high scores and high praise from professors.

Prior to the show's beginning House has a medical issue himself his leg muscle had died and had to be surgically removed, leading to him requiring a cane to get around, and continues throughout the show with severe pain problems. Further his injury catalyst a Vicodin addiction, supposed to be a tool for his chronic pain. Several times throughout the show House goes through withdrawal, some overdose scares, and other medical problems from the result of this addiction and disability.

House rarely follows medical intuition rules and federal laws, many times throughout the show House makes choices for the stake of the life of his patient, usually being illegal in some form. He coerces his team of three young doctors to complete unneeded tests, break into people's homes and go against the patients' desires to continue the path he believes is best, often accusing them of being stupid or suicidal to get them to agree to his whims. Though he is generally right about what is wrong with his patients, falters once or twice wanting to solve the mystery as fast as possible more than saving the patient's life in their best interests, in the end he still solves the case proving he is the best person for the job no matter his choices.

House continues to be loved throughout all seasons, his charming and reckless personality only making the show better, and his personal arch takes many forms. His struggles throughout the show how growth as a character is hard for him, taking leaps and bounds, nonetheless returning to his personality with a few small lessons gained along the way but never completely changing or growing. Always returning to his personal mantras of “people are idiots,” “always lie” and “don't change”, himself included.

Memorable Episodes

Season 1, Episode 21:House is asked to teach students, while trying to cure another patient presenting with a rash and some unexplainable symptoms to go along. The sub plot is House teaching these students, with three patients' cases coming into the hospital's free clinic with problems with their leg. One patient bitten by a snake, one who had run too much, injuring the muscle and one that was House's case, and how he became chronically in pain, causing the use of the cane throughout the series. The format of this episode was different to what viewers had seen so far from the show and is also memorable as viewer's finally get insight to house past.

Season 3, Episode 18:House and his team try to cure a choir that was exposed to a mysterious illness. The episode is memorable as it is the largest number of patients the team took on at once, usually only have been working with one patient at a time. The team struggled throughout the case to come to an agreement based on the abnormal symptoms the patients were presenting with. However, at the end the team figures it out and cures each patient.


Season 5, Episode 24: This episode is memorable because of the patients' impact on House and how the episode ends, setting up the beginning of the next season. The patient comes in with a rare disease that causes the two Halfs their brain to work independently. Leading to conflicting personal choices and personalities. Further as House is trying to grieve the death of his father, making him act out against the team and patient, at the end house commits himself to a mental asylum, believing he was hallucinating and going crazy.

Season 6, Episode 1 and 2: At the end of season five house commits himself to a mental hospital, believing to have been going crazy. The two-part episode that jumps starts season six in a different setting than House is usually seen in. Having completed vidicon withdrawal and forced to become a patient in residence House makes it difficult for the staff to try and help him. He fights with the Hosptial warden about his release and refuses to try and use the environment for self-betterment, only after meeting another patient and trying to help him, leading to serious injury and connecting with a patient's family member does he begin to accept help to get better and return home by the end of the second episode.
